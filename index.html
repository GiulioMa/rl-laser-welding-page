Reinforcement Learning on Reconfigurable Hardware

Overcoming Material Variability in Laser Material Processing

Giulio Masinelli, Chang Rajani, Patrik Hoffmann, Kilian Wasmer, David Atienza
Empa & EPFL | IEEE ICRA 2025
Paper Poster arXiv

Demo: our FPGA‑based RL policy adapts laser power on brushed, sand‑blasted, and mixed surfaces in real time.

Abstract

Ensuring consistent processing quality is challenging in laser processes due to varying material properties and surface conditions. We present a real‑time reinforcement‑learning controller implemented on an FPGA that adjusts laser power on the fly, learning directly from optical emissions without reward engineering or prior knowledge. Experiments on 316L stainless‑steel welds show up to 23 % higher rewards on rougher surfaces and 7 % on mixed surfaces compared with hand‑engineered constant‑power baselines, while maintaining conduction‑mode operation and avoiding defects.
